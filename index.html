Meghnad Saha (6th Oct 1893 - 16th Feb 1956)

1) Meghnad Saha was an Indian Astrophysicist who developed the Saha Ionization Equation which is used to describe the physical and chemical conditions in the stars. His work allowed astronomers to accurately relate the spectral classes of the stars to their actual temperatures.


2) He was elected to the Parliament of India in 1952.


3) He was a student of the Presidency College, Kolkata and Rajabazar Science College, CU; a professor at the Allahabad University from 1923 to 1938 and thereafter a Professor and Dean of the Faculty of Science at the University of Calcutta until his death in 1956.


4) He became the Fellow of the Royal Society in 1927.


5) He was the President of the 21st session of the Indian Science Congress in 1934.


6) He also invented an instrument to measure the weight and pressure of the solar rays and helped to build several scientific institutions.


7) He helped to build the Physics Department at Allahabad University and the Institute of Nuclear Physics at Kolkata.


8) He founded the journal Science and Culture and was his editor until his death.


9) He was the Director at Indian Association for the Cultivation of Science during 1953-1956. The Saha Institute of Nuclear Physics at Kolkata is named after him.


10) He was the chief architect of river planning in India and prepared the original plan for the Damodar Valley project.


11) His academic advisors were Jagadish Chandra Bose and Acharya Prafulla Chandra Ray. His Doctoral students were Samarendra Kumar Mitra and Daulat Singh Kothari.
